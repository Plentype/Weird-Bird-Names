Funny Bird Names

Everybody knows that birds come in all shapes and sizes. From miniature hummingbirds to gigantic ostriches, the Aves class distinguishes many-a-species. Unfortunately, some ended up with worse (funnier) names than others, and we will explore the top 10 most hilarious here.

#1. Blue Footed Booby

The Blue Footed Booby, despite its unfortunate name, is a beautiful creature with magical blue feet. With eyes like Medusa, this bird will stare into your soul and make you wonder where it all went wrong.

#2. Dickcissel

Dickcissels can typically be found in the prairies of North America. They are ground foragers that eat seeds, and when migrating flock in numbers in the millions.

#3. Go-Away-Bird

Go-Away-Birds live in the southern tropics of Africa. They are very common birds, and they got their name from the sound they make that sounds like "kweh" or "go-way". They have a length of around 47cm-51cm and a long iconic tail.

#4. Great Tit

The great tit is a distinctive bird with a black head and neck, prominent white cheeks, olive upperparts and yellow underparts, with some variation amongst the numerous subspecies. It is predominantly insectivorous in the summer, but will consume a wider range of food items in the winter months, including small hibernating bats.

#5. Horned Screamer

Horned screamers are the unicorns of the bird world. Over the course of their lives, these birds grow long, white spines of cartilage in the middle of their foreheads. Some birds possess horns approaching 15cm in length. No other birds on earth resemble it.

#6. Little Bustard

Little bustards are one of the larger birds on this list, despite what its name might suggest. Usually fairly quiet and unobtrusive, males preparing for breeding have distinctive plumage with a black neck and double V-shaped white collar. However, the population of these birds is decreasing.

#7. Red-Rumped Bush Tyrant

The red-rumped bush tyrant is a species of bird found in Bolivia, Colombia, Ecuador, and Peru. Its natural habitats are subtropical or tropical moist montane forests and subtropical or tropical high-altitude grassland. The adult red-rumped bush tyrant is about 23cm, and its body is covered in magical grey and brown colours camouflaging themselves with the surrounding wilderness.

#8. Satanic Nightjar

The Satanic nightjar, also known as Heinrich's nightjar, is a mid-sized, spotted, dark brown nightjar endemic to the Indonesian island of Sulawesi. It's around 27cm long and it has a white to buff-colored collar at its throat, blackish head, greyish-brown back, barred brown belly, and white spots on its 3rd and 4th outer primaries. It is considered a vulnerable to extinction because of it small population between 2500 to 9999 birds.

#9. Tiny Sky Tyrant

Tiny Sky Tyrants, also knows as Tiny Tyrant-Manakin, are small birds located in the subtropical and tropical jungles on South America, specifically in Brazil, French Guyana, Guyana, Suriname and Venezuela. Its binomial (biological) name is Tyranneutes virescens.

#10. Typical Swift

The Typical Swift, also known as the Common Swift, is a medium-sized bird, superficially similar to the barn swallow or house martin but somewhat larger. They are about 16-17cm, with a wingspan of 38-40cm. Much like their human counterparts, they fly high above other peoples' opinions and run away when the climate goes bad. They like to think they're unique, however there are more than 100 million specimens over the world. (Sorry Izabela)
